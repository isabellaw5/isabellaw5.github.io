About Me

Who I am

Hello! My name is Isabella Watson. I'm a 22 year old student in Chapel Hill, North Carolina.

What I'm Up To

I'm a junior majoring in biomedical engineering through both UNC Chapel Hill and the College of Engineering at NC State. I transferred to UNC in fall 2016 after earning my Associate in Science degree from Forsyth Technical Community College with highest honors. I'm also completing a minor in mathematics.
Outside of class, I work as a research intern for X2 Health, an initiative dedicated to improving women's healthcare in remote areas.
Expertise
Most of my experience lies in electrical engineering, with a focus on medical application. Two recent projects include a functional pulse oximeter and EKG system, both built from scratch (for under $30!). The “My Work” tab will link you to demonstrations of the aforementioned projects and more.

I also have a strong background using 3D CAD design software, predominately SolidWorks, to create printable objects. I work as the head designer for a non-profit organization, "Feelin' DNA", at UNC. "Feelin' DNA" is predicated on the idea that blind students deserve equal access to a solid STEM education. With this non-profit, I 3D model and print scientific models for blind elementary school-aged students. Examples are listed under the "My Work" tab.

During Fall semester 2017 I tackled my most challenging yet rewarding project: Designing, 3D printing, programming, and assembling a prosthetic arm for under $120. The prosthetic was commissioned by a real client and was customized as such.This project can be viewed under the "My Work" tab.

My work in electrical engineering has exposed me to the following: Hours of soldering, advanced laser-cutting, Adobe Illustrator design, 3D printing, microcontrollers, C and Python programming, Arduino, SolidWorks 3D CAD software, and more (see Resume).

Though I am most familiar with electronics, I am always eager to learn about and practice different specialties: BME aptly prepares its students with skills transferable to all engineering fields.
